The basics

Where is Rese available?

All 50 states. Our provider and pharmacy network can prescribe and ship nationwide — your assessment asks for your state so a provider licensed there reviews your case.

Who actually provides the clinical care?

Independent licensed providers in our contracted network — licensed in your state — make every clinical decision, from review to prescription to ongoing care. Rese itself doesn't practice medicine; your provider does.

Who fills and ships the prescriptions?

Licensed U.S. pharmacies, including state-regulated compounding pharmacies for compounded medications. Everything ships in discreet packaging.

Do I actually see a real doctor?

A licensed provider in your state reviews your full health history and makes every clinical decision — a real clinician, not an algorithm. In most states there's no appointment; a few require a quick video visit, and if yours does, we'll make it painless.

Is this legit?

Licensed providers, prescription medication through licensed pharmacies, and you’re never charged if you’re declined. Born from a real clinic in Gilbert, AZ — not a pitch deck.

What will I pay today?

Nothing is charged unless a licensed provider approves and prescribes treatment. At checkout your card is preauthorized for your exact stated price. You may see a temporary pending authorization from your bank — that is not a completed charge, and if treatment isn't approved it's released. Release timing varies by bank.

How fast does it ship?

Once your provider approves and prescribes, a licensed pharmacy fills your order and ships it with tracking, in discreet packaging. Exact timing varies by pharmacy and medication — you'll get your expected delivery window with your confirmation.

What if the provider says no?

You’re never charged. Your card is preauthorized at checkout and only charged once a provider approves your plan. That’s not the system failing — that's the system working.

Is compounded medication the same as the brand-name drug?

It contains the same active ingredient, prepared by a licensed compounding pharmacy. Compounded medications are not FDA-approved as finished drug products — a real distinction, and one we explain in plain English before checkout, never in fine print.

Is treatment guaranteed?

No — and be wary of anyone who says yes. Payment never purchases a prescription; a licensed provider decides what's medically appropriate. If the answer is no, you’re never charged.

How old do I have to be?

18 or older. We may ask for a photo and government ID during intake — state regulations require providers to verify who they're treating.

Will it work for me?

Honestly: results vary by individual, and anyone who promises otherwise is selling something. What we can promise is real medicine, a provider who monitors and adjusts your plan, and straight answers at every step — including when something isn't working.

The weight program

What does it cost?

Flat and public: semaglutide $185/mo, tirzepatide $299/mo. The price never rises with your dose, and the program price covers medication, provider care, check-ins, and shipping. No consult fees.

Why is the price flat when other programs charge by dose?

Because dose-based pricing punishes you for the medicine working as designed. Titration upward is normal and expected — your price stays the same at every dose, period.

What's the catch?

The closest thing to one: we ask for a three-month commitment when you start, to give your provider time to start treatment safely, evaluate your response, and adjust thoughtfully. You're still billed monthly. After three months: month-to-month, cancel in one step.

Is this cheating?

No. Needing insulin isn't cheating at diabetes, and quieting food noise isn't cheating at health. Your biology changed; your tools are allowed to change too.

Which medication is right for me?

That's your provider's call, made from your health history. You can state a preference in your assessment, and your provider will tell you honestly if the other one fits better — or if neither is safe for you.

How long until the medicine starts working?

Appetite effects often begin within the first couple of weeks; your provider titrates your dose gradually over several weeks to minimize side effects, and meaningful progress is typically evaluated around month three — which is exactly why we ask for the three-month start.

What about side effects?

The common ones are gastrointestinal — nausea early on, especially around dose increases. Your provider walks you through what to expect, and check-ins with real people mean side effects get managed, not endured. Anything severe: call 911 first, then us.

What happens when the scale slows?

Almost every body adapts between weeks 8 and 12 — it's biology, not failure. Our Plateau Protocol starts at week eight, before discouragement sets in. Read the full explanation →

Privacy & billing

Is my information private?

Yes. Your health information is protected under HIPAA, shipping is in discreet packaging, and we never contact your employer. Details in our Privacy Policy and HIPAA Notice.

Do you take insurance?

No — Rese is cash-pay, which is how we keep prices flat and public instead of wrapped in prior authorizations. Many patients use HSA/FSA funds; we can provide documentation for reimbursement requests.

When am I billed, and how do renewals work?

You see your exact price before you pay, and you're billed monthly. Your provider reviews your progress on an ongoing basis and, if appropriate, continues your prescription — dose adjustments never change your program price.

What if my package is lost or arrives damaged?

We'll make it right — replaced or refunded, your choice, at no cost. Prescription medication can't be returned once dispensed (pharmacy law), but our error is never your problem. Details in the Cancellation Policy.

How do I cancel?

In one step, from your account or by contacting us — a real person handles it, no retention script. Weight programs have a three-month initial commitment (billed monthly); everything after is month-to-month. Full terms in our Cancellation Policy.

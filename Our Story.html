Our Story

Before it was a company,
it was a family.

A nurse, a physician, and an operator — a daughter, her father, and her mother. Rese is what happened when they decided to build the care they kept wishing existed.

Where it began

A real clinic. Real patients. The same conversation, over and over.

It started in Gilbert, Arizona — in exam rooms, not a pitch deck.

For 25+ years, Dr. Smith practiced medicine the old-fashioned way: face to face. Before that he rode a fire engine as a firefighter/paramedic. The patients kept telling him the same story — exhausted, gaining weight, dismissed by a system with seven minutes to spare. Meanwhile his daughter Sabrina, a women's health nurse of 15+ years, was hearing the identical story from her patients. And living a version of it herself — until she finally found care that turned her own health around.

The medicine to help these people existed. What didn't exist was a way to get it that felt honest — clear prices, a licensed clinician making the call, and a human being answering when you have a question. So they built it. As a family, because that's the only way they know how to run things.

The name

Rese — pronounced REESE, rhymes with peace — is a four-generation family name.

It started with Lorese, Tera's grandmother. Tera Rese carried it next. Then her daughter, Sabrina Lorese. And now Sabrina's daughter, Aria Rese. Four generations, mother to daughter — today, the name of a company built for your health. Not invented. Inherited.

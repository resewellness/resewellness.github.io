Fair questions

Asked and answered

Is a prescription guaranteed?

No. A licensed provider reviews your health history and decides — and if the answer is no, they'll say so plainly and your card's preauthorization is released. You're never charged for medication that isn't approved.

What will I pay today?

Nothing is charged unless a licensed provider approves and prescribes treatment. At checkout your card is preauthorized for your program's flat monthly price — $185/mo semaglutide, $299/mo tirzepatide — and you see that exact number first. You may see a temporary pending authorization from your bank; that is not a completed charge. If treatment isn't approved, the authorization is released — timing varies by bank. [Confirm final wording with payment processor.]

Why is there a three-month care period?

It gives your provider time to start treatment safely, evaluate your response and tolerability, and adjust thoughtfully. You're billed monthly, and after three months it's month-to-month with one-step cancellation.

Who reviews my information?

A licensed provider in your state — a real clinician, not an algorithm. All clinical decisions are made by our licensed provider network.
